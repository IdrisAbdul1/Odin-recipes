Chia Seed Pudding

Description

Balsamic Vinaigrette is a tangy, slightly sweet dressing made by combining balsamic vinegar with oil, typically olive oil, and a blend of seasonings. It's a classic choice for salads, roasted vegetables, and marinades, offering a balance of acidity and richness that elevates dishes.

The base ratio is usually 1 part balsamic vinegar to 3 parts olive oil, but this can be adjusted depending on your preference for tanginess. Common additions include Dijon mustard, garlic, honey or maple syrup (for sweetness), salt, and freshly ground black pepper. The ingredients are whisked or shaken together until emulsified, creating a smooth, pourable dressing.

Ingredients

  • 1/2 cup balsamic vinegar
  • 1/4 cup extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon Dijon mustard
  • 2 cloves garlic, crushed
  • 1 teaspoon maple syrup (optional for sweetness)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Steps

  1. In a small bowl or jar, combine the balsamic vinegar, olive oil, Dijon mustard, and maple syrup (if using).
  2. Whisk or shake well to combine until emulsified.
  3. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  4. Serve immediately or store in the refrigerator for up to a week. Shake well before using.
